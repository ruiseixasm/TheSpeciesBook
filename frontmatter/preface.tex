\chapter{Preface}

This book began as a question: What does it mean for a species to have a will? 
The idea first came to me while observing a colony of ants building a bridge 
with their own bodies. Was this instinct? Programming? Or something deeper?

Over the following years, the question expanded. If a species has a will, 
can that will be opposed? Can it be defeated? And if so, what are the 
implications for humanity's place in the natural order?

This work is the result of that inquiry. It is not a biology textbook, 
though it draws on biological observation. It is not a work of philosophy, 
though it engages with philosophical questions. It is, perhaps, a meditation 
on the tension between the individual and the collective, the momentary and 
the eternal.

I am grateful to those who read early drafts and offered critique, 
especially those who disagreed. They sharpened the argument.

Rui Monteiro
Lisbon, 2026
