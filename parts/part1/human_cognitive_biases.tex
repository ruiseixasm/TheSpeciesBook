\chapter{Human Cognitive Biases}

\section{Romanticism}

Romanticism is the root of all benign interpretations of reality, all religions are a form
of Romanticism, where reality is interpreted as a benevolent environment centered on the
believer where the common experience is answered in a faithful fashion.

This is a bias in the way that ideas that don't fir this framework instinctively become denied,
a good example of it is climate change, where a hopeless reality finds denial before any
consideration.

Other insidious problem with romanticism, is the sense of belonging that comes with, like
with religion, a given established interpretation of reality triggers the ownership feeling,
where anything that contradicts that interpretation is felt as a lost, the best example of it,
was the idea of the Sun as the center of the solar system, where the already owned idea needed
to be discarded with huge costs for being so deep rooted in the common religion.
